Future plans

Globe.ly events which are usually organized as volunteering, nature-oriented actions, emphasizes a wish to embrace people’s awareness and caring for nature, and turn it into a global move that will start our planet’s recovery. Such events previously took places near our main headquarter London, starting small and turned big. From cleaning parks and planting trees to gathering plastic and recycling. Due recently made partnership with Seventh Generation and New Belgium Brewing we managed to expand our activity area even more. Future plans include greater events with bigger number of volunteers. Making San Francisco and New York City as new regional headquarters in the US is currently in the process. Therefore, soon it will be possible for you to be involved in our events, if you were previously too far away, but wanted to participate physically.

How to join

As already mentioned, Globe.ly organizes volunteering events at most. Every event is divided into few different tasks which are later given to certain groups. Actions that take risk are strictly excluded. Lowest age limit for participating is 14, otherwise the presence of an adult is required. No previous knowledge nor skills are needed. If you would like to help, but have no intentions in taking part of our events, you can always check other ways that will be taken into consideration. Also, donating is one more way to help our organization to make a difference to our environment. All donations are used for buying proper equipment for Globe.ly volunteering events.

Le Havre, France

Cutting grass and taking care of the Le Havre's most visited park were important due often activity of their citizens. Fun family and friends gatherings are not a rare case there.

Domburg, Netherlands

In 2018, we made a powerful presentation about the global warming and the importance of preventing climate changes. Soon, Domburg became one of our regional headquarters in Europe.

Barcelona, Spain

One of our first ogrganized beach cleanings took place at one of the biggest and most crowded cities in Europe. More than 100 people and children participated.

Pula, Croatia

One of our many tasks at Adriatic coastline was taking care of Pula and its beaches. Croatia draws many tourists over year and we made sure to encourage them to join us as well.

North Devon, England

Cleaning Ilfracombe and Woolacombe beaches with more than 100 people involved. Event awoke citizents about the importance of a proper taking care of their beaches.
